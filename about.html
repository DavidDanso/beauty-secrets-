The Founder

Francesca Brenda Opoku

Francesca Brenda Opoku is the founder and CEO of Solution Oasis the company that owns the Beauty Secrets skincare line. She is a University of Ghana Business School graduate and an IVLP alumna. She grew up in Northern Ghana where holistic and traditional cosmetics are predominant. Growing up she recounts watching women harvest and process Shea nuts with herbs into cosmetic products that were effective. In Northern Ghana, Shea nuts are known as “women’s gold”. She eventually developed the skill of making Shea butter and other natural cosmetic products. She saw the wonders that the Shea butter did to the skin of the women and their skin under the sweltering climate in Ghana. Our Mission

At Beauty Secrets Africa, we believe everyone is beautiful naturally, our products only reveal this beauty. Our aim is to deliver effective and healthy skin care to everyone regardless of age, sex, race etc. We are committed to offering our clients the very best products, giving them superior value for their money. We work at providing fair prices for all stakeholders in our production, the women who supply us with natural ingredients; our employees and all who offer any product or service.

Our Process

Our aim is to produce premium organic skincare products for all age and skin type. At Beauty Secrets, we combine a variety of plant-based materials to manufacture quality skincare products. From acceptance of raw materials to delivery, we guarantee a smooth cosmetic manufacture. Every stage of production is under safe, quality standards and FDA approved procedures that is guaranteed to give results. We integrate the advanced cosmetic production with indigenous procedures to offer skincare solutions to our cherished customers. All parts of our value chain is sourced locally from Ghana.

Our Impact

Our inspiration comes from women all over this continent who work tirelessly to preserve beauty secrets in Africa; especially our women in Tamale who supply our shea butter.

We actively work to maximise social impact throughout the entire supply chain. This span from the women in Tamale who supply our shea butter all the way to the young pupils who help us package our goods to finance their education.
